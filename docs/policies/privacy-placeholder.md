# Placeholder Privacy Policy

> Documentation note: this is a product/legal-readiness draft only. It is not legal advice and must be reviewed by a qualified lawyer before public launch.

## Purpose

This starter Privacy page describes the first-beta data categories and safety workflows that appear in the app.

## Placeholder coverage

- account and profile data
- public trade/profile content
- proposal/support/report data
- uploaded images and screenshots
- moderation, restriction, and audit records
- settings such as language, appearance, country, and display currency

## Data-readiness notes

- Public profile and active public content can appear in discovery/profile routes.
- Proposal messages, support tickets, report escalations, and admin notes are not public discovery content.
- Uploads may be retained while needed for product function, support, safety review, or audit history.
- Optional-auth public reads should gracefully fall back to anonymous access when stale tokens are present.

## Lawyer-review questions

- Which GDPR/ePrivacy/UK GDPR/CCPA or other regional obligations apply at launch?
- What user rights flow is needed for access, export, correction, deletion, and objection?
- What retention periods should be documented for images, support tickets, reports, audit logs, and sessions?
- What processor/subprocessor list is needed for hosting, email, analytics, storage, crash reporting, and future payment providers?
- What App Store / Play Store privacy disclosures must match the implementation?
